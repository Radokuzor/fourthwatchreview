What Happened to Your Amazon Seller Account?

Amazon suspensions happen fast and without warning. You log in and find your account deactivated, your listings pulled, and your Amazon funds frozen. The suspension notice is vague, the appeal process is confusing, and every day your account is offline you're losing revenue and ranking history.

Amazon suspends accounts for two main reasons: performance issues (order defect rate, late shipment rate, valid tracking rate) and policy violations (IP complaints, review manipulation, listing abuse, restricted products). Each requires a completely different appeal strategy.

Amazon's Seller Performance team receives thousands of appeals daily. A generic Plan of Action or one that doesn't directly address the root cause will be rejected — often permanently. Getting this right the first time matters more than getting it done fast.

Common Reasons Amazon Sellers Get Suspended

Each suspension type requires a different approach — knowing which one you have is critical

Poor Account Health Metrics

Order Defect Rate above 1%, Late Shipment Rate above 4%, or Valid Tracking Rate below 95% triggers Amazon's performance suspension policy.

Intellectual Property Complaints

IP infringement, trademark, or copyright complaints from rights holders can suspend individual ASINs or your entire selling account.

Review Manipulation

Incentivizing reviews, review swapping, or receiving a competitor's false report of review manipulation can trigger an immediate account suspension.

Counterfeit / Inauthentic Product Claims

Customer or brand complaints about counterfeit or inauthentic products — even if your products are genuine — require strong invoice documentation to resolve.

Listing Policy Violations

Listing restricted products, variations abuse, title manipulation, or prohibited product claims can all trigger ASIN or account-level deactivations.

Section 3 / Related Account Violations

Amazon's Section 3 violations — including operating multiple accounts without authorization — are serious and require a carefully structured appeal to address.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Plan of Action and why does it matter?
A Plan of Action (POA) is a structured appeal document Amazon requires for reinstatement. It must clearly identify the root cause of the suspension, the corrective actions you've taken, and your prevention plan. Amazon rejects vague, emotional, or poorly structured POAs — format and specificity matter enormously.
How long does Amazon seller reinstatement take?
Amazon typically responds to well-prepared appeals within 7–21 days. Complex cases — IP violations, counterfeit claims, multiple ASINs, or repeated appeals — can take 4–12 weeks. We give you a realistic estimate after your diagnostic, not a sales pitch timeline.
My first Plan of Action was rejected. Can you still help?
Yes — but the window narrows with each rejected appeal. We analyze what went wrong with your previous POA, identify what Amazon's team is actually looking for, and rebuild the appeal from the ground up with the right structure and evidence.
What's the difference between an account suspension and ASIN suspension?
An ASIN suspension deactivates a specific product listing but leaves your account active. An account suspension takes your entire selling account offline. Both require appeals, but account suspensions are more urgent and generally more complex to resolve. We handle both.
Can I access my Amazon funds while suspended?
Amazon typically holds your disbursements for 90 days after a suspension. In some cases funds are held longer or subject to deductions for refunds and claims. We can advise on the fund release process as part of your recovery plan.
